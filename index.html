How Much Should You Feed Your Dog?

Determining the right amount of food for your dog depends on several factors including their size, age, metabolism, and activity level. Our calculator provides a general guideline, but always consult with your veterinarian for personalized advice.

Overfeeding can lead to obesity and related health problems, while underfeeding may result in nutritional deficiencies. It's important to monitor your dog's weight and adjust portions accordingly.

Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I feed my dog?

Puppies typically need 3-4 small meals per day. Adult dogs do well with 2 meals per day, while some seniors may prefer smaller, more frequent meals. The total daily amount calculated by our tool can be divided into these meals.

Why does spaying/neutering affect food requirements?

Spayed or neutered dogs often have slower metabolisms and may require 20-30% fewer calories than intact dogs. This is why our calculator adjusts recommendations based on this factor.

Should I follow the feeding guidelines on dog food packages?

Package guidelines are a good starting point but are often too general. Our calculator provides more personalized recommendations by considering your dog's specific characteristics. Always monitor your dog's weight and condition and adjust as needed.
